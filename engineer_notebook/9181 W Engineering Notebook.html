Competition Analysis

What the competition is:

This year's competition is called “Tower Takeover” the objective is to score as many points as possible in a 2 minute period. This 2 minute period is broken up into 2 sections: autonomous period and the driver control period. The autonomous period lasts for 15 seconds at the start of the match and during this time the robots may only be controlled by a program. If one team has a higher score after this period they are awarded 6 points before driver control begins. Driver control then lasts 1:45 before the game ends. Once the game ends the referee’s then tally up the score and the results are announced.

The game objects for this seasons competition are as follows:

  • 66 cubes (5.5” x 5.5” x 5.5”)
  • There are 3 colours of cube
  • 22 purple
  • 22 orange
  • 22 green
  • There are 7 towers in total
  • 1 tall tower ()
  • 6 shorter towers ()
    • 2 of which are team specific

    How this seasons game is played :

    This main objective of this years game is to place cubes in your zone and increase the value of those cubes by placing cubes of corresponding colour in towers. Each cube that is placed properly in your zone is worth 1 point on its own. Cubes placed in towers are worth 0 points inherently however each cube of corresponding colour in your zone is worth +1 point. For example, if I had 9 cubes (3 orange, 1 green, 5 purple) in my zone that would be worth only 9 points but if there were 2 purple cubes in towers each purple cube would now be worth 2 points increasing my score from 9 to 14. Because of the intricate game design, it will be imperative to monitor what colour of cubes are placed in the towers and it will be crucial to make sure you use your team-specific tower to its utmost efficiency.

    Initial speculation on different strategies and how they will affect gameplay:

    There are many strategies that become apparent once you get a basic understanding of the game and these ideas may change throughout the season and may end up being sub-par.

    In this season the autonomous period will be very important because of the point bonus and the introduction of purple cubes. We initially thought that each team would pick a specific role for autonomous by having one member score cubes in towers and the other score cubes in the zone, however this may not end up being that practical being that we have 15 seconds in autonomous and it can be used to do a variety of things.

    Our initial thought for driver control was that one robot would work on the towers (scoring and de-scoring) and the other would work on putting high stacks of cubes in the zones. After the season begun we realized that robots could be well designed to achieve both functions in a short amount of time and it is more practical to have each robot function in its own quadrant of the field.

    During driver and autonomous skills strategies are very different from competition strategies.

    Design Thoughts And Process

    Initial thoughts on design and required functions for a robot:

    This years robots have to do 3 things: score cubes in zones, score cubes in towers, and remove cubes from towers. There are multiple different systems on a robot that can be used in this competition to maximize the ability to stack cubes and place them in towers. First of all, you could use a modified double reverse 4-bar to pick up multiple cubes at a time and place them on other stacks, with this system it is possible to place cubes in towers efficiently and quickly. Second, you could use some precision system to pick up a bunch of cubes at a time and place them all at once in one zone, by picking up so many cubes at a time it is unlikely that this system will be able to place cubes in towers without the assistance of another part of the robot. Lastly, Claw Bot . In the case of the robot that picks up many cubes at a time an addition might need to be added for the robot to be able to score and de-score cubes in towers.
